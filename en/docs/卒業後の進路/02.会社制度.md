<h1 style="text-align: center;">Employment Types</h1>

<span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70"></span>

In job hunting, as much attention should be paid to a <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">company’s employment system</span> as to salary or job responsibilities. This is not just an internal set of rules; it forms the foundation for an employee’s `financial stability`, `skill development`, and `future career options`.

### <div class="relative font-bold text-2xl pb-3 after:absolute after:left-0 after:bottom-0 after:w-full after:border-b-4 after:border-dotted after:border-blue-600">Employment Types</div>

Employment type refers to the kind of employment contract between a company and its employees.

- The contract period varies depending on the employment type.
- Compensation, benefits, and other conditions differ by employment type.
- Working hours and work style also vary

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8 text-center'>Simple description</p>

| Employment Type   | Contract Period              | Social Stability / Credibility | Salary & Benefits              | Scope of Responsibility       |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------- | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------ | ----------------------------- |
| Regular Employee  | Permanent (until retirement) | ✓✓✓✓✓                          | High (with raises and bonuses) | Broad (higher responsibility) |
| Contract Employee | Fixed-term (up to 3 years)   | ✓✓✓                            | Medium (often no bonuses)      | Limited and defined           |
| Temporary Staff   | Fixed-term (up to 3 years)   | ✓✓                             | Hourly wage (paid by agency)   | Within assigned tasks         |
| Part-time         | Fixed-term / Permanent       | ✓                              | Hourly wage (shift-based)      | Supportive / limited          |

<br>

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>Regular Employee</p>

| Item                       | Details                                         |
| -------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Employment Type            | `Regular Employee` (Permanent Employment)       |
| Contract Period            | No fixed term (`Permanent`)                     |
| Working Hours              | Generally `full-time`                           |
| Salary & Benefits          | `Established` salary, bonuses, and raise system |
| Social Insurance & Welfare | `Comprehensive` benefits                        |
| Stability                  | `High`                                          |
| Responsibility & Scope     | Relatively `broad`                              |

<br>

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>Contract Employee</p>

| Item                          | Details                                                        |
| ----------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Employment Type               | `Contract` Employee (Fixed-term Employment)                    |
| Contract Period               | `Fixed-term` (generally up to 3 years, renewal not guaranteed) |
| Job Duties & Scope            | `Clearly` defined                                              |
| Salary & Benefits             | Often more `limited` than those of regular employees           |
| Possibility of Regularization | May be `possible` based on performance or evaluation           |

<br>

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>Temporary Staff</p>

| Item                   | Details                                       |
| ---------------------- | --------------------------------------------- |
| Employment Type        | `Temporary Staff`                             |
| Employment Contract    | Concluded with the `staffing agency`          |
| Workplace              | Actual work location is the `client company`  |
| Contract Period        | Generally up to `3 years`                     |
| Working Hours & Duties | Follow `instructions` from the client company |
| Salary                 | Paid by the `staffing agency`                 |
| Responsibilities       | Often `specialized` or `supportive` tasks     |

<br>

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>Part-time</p>

| Item                        | Details                                          |
| --------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| Employment Type             | `Part-time` / Casual (Non-regular Employment)    |
| Work Style                  | `Short hours`, fewer days per week               |
| Purpose of Work             | Often to `balance` with family or studies        |
| Employment Contract         | Often `fixed-term`                               |
| Benefits & Social Insurance | Eligibility depends on `hours` and `days` worked |
| Responsibility & Scope      | Relatively `limited`                             |

<br>

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>Feature and benefit for each employment type</p>

| Item               | Regular Employee                                    | Contract Employee                        | Temporary Staff                                | Part-time / Casual                             |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------- |
| Benefits           | `Comprehensive` social insurance & welfare          | Some benefits `provided`                 | `Limited`, usually provided by staffing agency | `Partially` applicable depending on conditions |
| Responsibility     | `Broad responsibility` for overall work and results | Responsibility for `assigned tasks`      | `Limited` scope, lighter responsibility        | `Limited` scope, lighter responsibility        |
| Promotion / Raises | `Well-established system`                           | `Limited`, possibility of regularization | Generally `none`                               | Generally `none`                               |
| Job Stability      | `High`                                              | `Medium`                                 | `Low`                                          | `Low`                                          |

<br>

### <div class="relative font-bold text-2xl pb-3 after:absolute after:left-0 after:bottom-0 after:w-full after:border-b-4 after:border-dotted after:border-blue-600">Recruitment Type</div>

Recruitment Type refers to the framework of hiring that considers the job category and potential career path at the time of employment
<br>

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>Management-Track Position</p>

| Item           | Details                                                                                                   |
| -------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Hiring Premise | Hired with the expectation of gaining future `managerial roles` or experience in `multiple job functions` |
| Assignment     | `Not limited` at entry; often involves rotations through sales, planning, administration, etc.            |
| Career Path    | Likely to be eligible for promotions and raises; career path is `clearly` defined                         |

<br>

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>Specialized Staff</p>

| Item               | Details                                                                             |
| ------------------ | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Hiring Premise     | Hired with the expectation of `specializing` in a `specific task` or `job function` |
| Assignment & Scope | Relatively `fixed`; transfers or department changes are less likely                 |
| Career Path        | `Limited` opportunities for promotion; not as broad as generalist positions         |

<br>

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>Technical Position</p>

| Item               | Details                                                                                                |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Hiring Premise     | Hired to work in a `specialized technical` or `professional field`                                     |
| Assignment & Scope | Relatively `fixed`; few transfers or department rotations                                              |
| Career Path        | Career progression focuses on `skill mastery`; promotion scope is `narrower` than generalist positions |

### <div class="relative font-bold text-2xl pb-3 after:absolute after:left-0 after:bottom-0 after:w-full after:border-b-4 after:border-dotted after:border-blue-600">Job Training</div>

Training refers to an organized `educational program` conducted by a company to systematically and efficiently help employees—ranging from new hires to managers—acquire the knowledge and skills necessary for their work.

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>OJT（On Job Training）</p>

| Item               | Details                                                                                  |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Characteristics    | Practical training conducted on the `job`                                                |
| Instruction Method | Direct guidance from `senior employees` or `supervisors` to acquire skills and knowledge |
| Purpose & Effect   | Acquire skills and problem-solving abilities `directly applicable to work`               |

<br>

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>Off-JT（Off-the-Job Training）</p>

| Item             | Details                                                   |
| ---------------- | --------------------------------------------------------- |
| Characteristics  | Training conducted `off-the-job`, away from the workplace |
| Learning Content | Learning `industry knowledge` and `specific skills`       |

<br>

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>collective training</p>

| Item             | Details                                                                                                            |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Participants     | `All new employees`                                                                                                |
| Learning Content | Acquire essential `basic knowledge`, `business manners`, and `company culture` necessary for working professionals |

<br>

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800 decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>Mentor System</p>

| Item     | Details                                                                                                |
| -------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Role     | `Experienced employees` (mentors) provide `advice` and `guidance` to junior or new employees (mentees) |
| Benefits | Creates an environment where mentees can `consult freely` and accelerates `understanding of work`      |
