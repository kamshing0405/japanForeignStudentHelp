To study in Japan, two items are needded: a <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">passport issued by your home country</span> and a <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">visa</span> issued by the Government of Japan (embassy or consulate).
International students usually obtain the <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">“Student”</span> status of residence. Unlike tourism, studying in Japan requires obtaining a <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">Certificate of Eligibility (COE)</span>.

<br>

### <div class="relative font-bold text-2xl pb-3 after:absolute after:left-0 after:bottom-0 after:w-full after:border-b-4 after:border-dotted after:border-blue-600">Visa</div>

<span class="bg-blue-500 w-48 px-3 py-1 rounded-2xl text-4 text-sm text-white">Approximately 2 to 4 months in total</span>

The general process for applying for a student visa is as follows:

| Process            | Contents                                                                                                                                       |
| ------------------ | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Admission Approval | Receive an `acceptance letter` (certificate of admission) from a Japanese educational institution                                              |
| COE Application    | The school `applies on your behalf` to Immigration for a `Certificate of Eligibility`<br> (the review process takes approximately `1–3 months` |
| COE Issuance       | After approval, the `COE` is issued and sent to the applicant                                                                                  |
| Visa Application   | Submit the required documents, including the COE, at a `Japanese embassy or consulate`                                                         |
| Arrival in Japan   | Enter Japan within the `validity period` of the COE after obtaining the visa                                                                   |

<p class='text-dark-white font-bold underline decoration-red-800  decoration-4 underline-offset-8'>The Importance of the Certificate of Eligibility (COE)</p>

| Item            | Details                                                                                                                                                                                                        |
| --------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Role            | A document that proves the applicant meets entry requirements, such as a `study plan` and `financial capability`. Submitting the COE when applying for a visa can `significantly shorten` the screening period |
| Validity Period | In principle, the applicant must `enter Japan within three months` after the visa is issued. If the deadline is exceeded, the COE may become `invalid`                                                         |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">Issuance of the Residence Card</div>

| Item               | Details                                                                                                                                             |
| ------------------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Landing Permission | At immigration inspection at the `airport`, a `landing permission` sticker is affixed to the passport                                               |
| Residence Card     | At major airports such as New Chitose, Narita, Haneda, Chubu, Kansai, Hiroshima, and Fukuoka, the `Residence Card is issued on the spot` upon entry |
| Important Notes    | The Residence Card is the `only official form of identification` for foreign residents in Japan and must be `carried at all times`                  |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">Required Procedures After Arrival</div>

| Item                 | Details                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Address Registration | After securing a residence, bring your `Residence Card` and complete address registration at the `municipal office (city/ward office)` within `14 days`                                    |
| My Number            | After address registration, a `My Number notification card` will be sent by mail. It is `required` for procedures such as `opening a bank account` and `tax procedures for part-time work` |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">Permission to Engage in Activity Other Than That Permitted</div>

| Item        | Details                                                                                                                                                                  |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Principle   | Under the `Student` status of residence, `employment is generally prohibited`                                                                                            |
| Application | If you wish to work part-time, you must apply for `Permission to Engage in Activity Other Than That Permitted`                                                           |
| Time Limit  | Up to `28 hours per week` <br> During long vacations (e.g. summer break): up to `8 hours per day`                                                                        |
| Key Point   | If you submit the application form at `immigration inspection upon entry`, permission can be `granted on the spot`, eliminating the need to visit the Immigration Office |
