To support international students living in Japan, various forms of assistance are provided by the national government, local municipalities, and public organizations. These are broadly categorized into <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">five</span> main areas: `Finance (Scholarships) & Housing`, `Life & Legal Consultation`, `Japanese Language Study`, `Career`, and `General Information`.

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">Finance (Scholarships & Financial Support)</div>

These initiatives are primarily led by the Japanese government (<span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">MEXT</span>) and the <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">Japan Student Services Organization (JASSO)</span>. Scholarships are generally divided into four application routes. It is important to understand the characteristics of each to find the one that best suits your needs.

Securing "Housing" for job hunting or further education is a <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">foundation of daily life</span>. For international students, we have organized the main options and support systems available to help you find a safe place to live while keeping the financial burden low.

#### <div class="custom-title">Career</div>

When international students begin job hunting (career building) in Japan, the first point of contact should be public services operated by the national or local governments. These are completely free and provide wide-ranging support from <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">resume correction and mock interviews</span> to <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">visa consultation</span>.
