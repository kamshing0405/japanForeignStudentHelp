Housing options for international students include `university dormitories`, `share houses`, `student apartments`, and `private rental apartments`. While dorms managed by universities or local municipalities are affordable and well-equipped, private apartments remain the mainstream choice, used by approximately <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">80%</span> of international students.

The keys to a smooth search and a comfortable life in Japan are: consulting with your university's International Student Office, utilizing properties that do not require a guarantor or come furnished, and understanding Japanese customs such as `Reikin` (Key Money) and `Shikikin` (Security Deposit).

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">4 Main Housing Options (For Reference)</div>

The point is to choose based on your personality and budget.
<br>

| Type              | Estimated Cost   | Pros                                                          | Cons                                                           |
| ----------------- | ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------- |
| University Dorm   | ¥30,000–¥60,000  | `Cheap`, simple procedures, easy to make friends.             | `Curfews` and `rules`, many shared areas.                      |
| Share House       | ¥45,000–¥80,000  | `Furnished`, often `no guarantor needed`, social interaction. | `Less privacy`, `strict` cleaning rules.                       |
| Student Apartment | ¥60,000–¥90,000  | `Safe` (student-only), `excellent` facilities.                | Requires `screening`, more expensive than standard apartments. |
| Private Apartment | ¥40,000–¥100,000 | Complete `freedom`, choose your `preferred location`.         | `High initial costs`, strict guarantor/screening process.      |

#### <div class="custom-title">Japanese Rules You Should Know</div>

<div class="flex items-center text-center gap-2 h-6 mt-6">
    <span class="flex items-center justify-center w-6 h-6 bg-[#00873c] text-white rounded-full text-sm font-bold shrink-0">1</span>
    <p class="text-[#00873c] font-bold text-lg pl-0.5">Initial Costs</p>
</div>

In Japan, you often need to pay an upfront cost equivalent to <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">3 to 5 months of rent</span>.

1. `Shikikin`(Security Deposit): A deposit used for cleaning or repairs when you move out.

2. `Reikin`(Key Money): A non-refundable "gratitude" payment to the landlord. Recently, "Zero Reikin" properties are increasing.
   <br>

<div class="flex items-center text-center gap-2 h-6 mt-6">
    <span class="flex items-center justify-center w-6 h-6 bg-[#00873c] text-white rounded-full text-sm font-bold shrink-0">2</span>
    <p class="text-[#00873c] font-bold text-lg pl-0.5">Guarantor Companies</p>
</div>

Since most international students do not have a `Joint Guarantor` in Japan, it is common to pay a `Guarantor Company` (such as GTN, which specializes in foreigners) to provide this service.

<br>

<div class="flex items-center text-center gap-2 h-6 mt-6">
    <span class="flex items-center justify-center w-6 h-6 bg-[#00873c] text-white rounded-full text-sm font-bold shrink-0">3</span>
    <p class="text-[#00873c] font-bold text-lg pl-0.5">Renewal Fee</p>
</div>

When renewing a lease (usually every `2 years`), a Renewal Fee (`Koushin-ryo`) equivalent to about one month's rent is often required.
<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">Tips for Wise Housing Selection</div>

<div class="flex items-center text-center gap-2 h-6 mt-6">
    <span class="flex items-center justify-center w-6 h-6 bg-[#00873c] text-white rounded-full text-sm font-bold shrink-0">1</span>
    <p class="text-[#00873c] font-bold text-lg pl-0.5">To Save on Initial Costs</p>
</div>

Furnished "Share Houses" or "University Dorms" are best. Not having to buy a refrigerator or washing machine can <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">save you hundreds of thousands of yen</span>.

<br>

<div class="flex items-center text-center gap-2 h-6 mt-6">
    <span class="flex items-center justify-center w-6 h-6 bg-[#00873c] text-white rounded-full text-sm font-bold shrink-0">2</span>
    <p class="text-[#00873c] font-bold text-lg pl-0.5">Consider Commuting Costs</p>
</div>

Even if the rent is cheap, if the room is far from school, your train fare (<span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">Commuter Pass</span>) will be high. Judge by the total of rent plus transportation.

<br>
