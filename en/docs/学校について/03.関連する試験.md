For students aiming to enter Japanese higher education institutions, it is usually necessary to take several <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">important examinations</span>. These exams assess not only <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">Japanese language proficiency</span> but also fundamental <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">academic skills</span>.

### <div class="relative font-bold text-2xl pb-3 after:absolute after:left-0 after:bottom-0 after:w-full after:border-b-4 after:border-dotted after:border-blue-600">Main Examination</div>

#### <div class="custom-title">Examination for Japanese University Admission for International Students（EJU）</div>

The [EJU](https://www.jasso.go.jp/ryugaku/eju/index.html) is a test designed for foreign students who wish to enter Japanese universities (undergraduate programs, etc.). Its purpose is to assess the <span class="font-bold bg-linear-to-t from-yellow-400/70 from-10% to-transparent to-50% dark:from-green-400/70">Japanese language proficiency and basic academic skills</span> required by Japanese universities.

| Item          | Details                                                              |
| ------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Test Period   | Annually in `June` and `November`                                    |
| Japanese      | `Reading, Listening, and Writing`                                    |
| Science       | Choose `2 subjects` from Physics, Chemistry, and Biology             |
| Comprehensive | `Politics, Economics, Geography, History`                            |
| Mathematics   | `Course 1` for humanities; `Course 2` for sciences                   |
| Importance    | Used by many universities as `an important reference for admissions` |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">Japanese-Language Proficiency Test（JLPT）</div>

The [Japanese-Language Proficiency Test (JLPT)](https://www.jlpt.jp) is the world's largest Japanese language certification exam, divided into five levels from N1 to N5.

| Item               | Details                                                                                |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Test Period        | Every year in `July` and `December`                                                    |
| Use for Admission  | Vocational schools often require `N2` or higher; graduate schools usually require `N1` |
| Use for Employment | Many companies prefer candidates with `N1` certification                               |
| Benefits           | No expiration date and `recognized internationally`                                    |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">English Proficiency Test</div>

For admission to prestigious university faculties (e.g., Waseda, Keio, former Imperial Universities) or graduate schools (especially in science, engineering, economics, and medical fields), it is often required to submit English proficiency test scores.

| Test Name                                       | Features                                                            |
| ----------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| [TOEFL](https://www.toefl-ibt.jp)               | Widely recognized and `accepted by almost all universities`         |
| [TOEIC](https://www.iibc-global.org/toeic.html) | Highly valued in Japan, `useful for business and practical fields`  |
| [IELTS](https://www.eiken.or.jp/ielts/)         | Recognition is increasing, but `usage in Japan is somewhat limited` |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">College / University Entrance Exams</div>

This is an exam that each university conducts independently after passing the document screening and EJU selection process.

| Item         | Details                                                                                      |
| ------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Written Exam | Evaluates logical thinking and expression through `essay` and `specialized subjects`         |
| Interview    | Focuses on motivation for studying abroad, study plans, and `Japanese conversational skills` |
| Importance   | Especially important as the `final selection` that determines acceptance                     |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">Other Examination</div>

| Exam Name                                      | Details                                                                                                          |
| ---------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| National Center Test for University Admissions | Most international students are `not required` to take it, but it may be necessary for some fields like medicine |
| Practical Exam for Arts                        | Conducted at art/music universities, such as `drawing or performance`                                            |

<br>

### <div class="relative font-bold text-2xl pb-3 after:absolute after:left-0 after:bottom-0 after:w-full after:border-b-4 after:border-dotted after:border-blue-600">Important points to note regarding the exam</div>

When taking exams in Japan (especially for universities, graduate schools, or specialized training colleges), there are several unique customs and rules to be aware of. The following points summarize common mistakes that international students or those living abroad often make.

#### <div class="custom-title">Document preparation</div>

| Point of Attention      | Details                                                                                                                                |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Submission of Originals | Graduation and transcript certificates must be submitted as `originals`. Copies are often not accepted                                 |
| Translation Attachment  | Documents in languages other than Japanese or English require an official translation (`notarized`)                                    |
| Validity Check          | EJU, TOEFL, and IELTS scores are generally `valid for 2 years`. Also, pay attention to documents specified as issued within `3 months` |
| Consistent Name Format  | Ensure that the spelling of your name `matches` across passport, certificates, and transcripts                                         |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">Application deadline and mailing</div>

| Point of Attention | Details                                                                                                      |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Arrival / Postmark | `Arrival`: Must reach the university by the deadline<br>`Postmark`: Postmarked by the deadline is acceptable |
| Mailing Method     | Methods such as `registered mail` or `Letter Pack` are often required for tracking purposes                  |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">Exam application time</div>

| Period       | Exam / Procedure  | Notes                                                                        |
| ------------ | ----------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| March & Sept | EJU Application   | For exams held in June & November                                            |
| March & Aug  | JLPT Registration | For exams held in July & December                                            |
| All year     | TOEFL / TOEIC     | Sending score reports may take about `1 month`; early testing is recommended |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">EJU Subject Selection</div>

| Point            | Details                                                                                                     |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Choice of Field  | Select `Humanities` or `Science` according to the requirements of the desired school                        |
| Language of Exam | Check whether the exam is in `Japanese` or `English` (Japanese-taught departments usually require Japanese) |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">Interview etiquette</div>

| Point            | Details                                                                                           |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Attire           | Wear `suits` or other clothing with a `neat appearance`. Jeans and sandals are prohibited.        |
| Punctuality      | Arrive at the venue or online `15 minutes early`.                                                 |
| Motivation Essay | Be prepared to explain the `reason for choosing the university/department` clearly and logically. |

<br>

#### <div class="custom-title">Exam fee payment</div>

| Point            | Details                                                                      |
| ---------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Proof of Payment | Do not forget to `attach the payment receipt` to your application documents. |
